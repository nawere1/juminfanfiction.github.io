Baby, It's Cold Outside

Sitting up to look in your eyes, Jumin began: “Perhaps the holiday atmosphere has infiltrated my mind and as such, I’d much rather extend this romantic evening with my beautiful girlfriend than send her out into a raging blizzard. Wouldn’t you agree, Y/N? The roads are terrible and it’s freezing out there!”

You sat and stared at him for a moment, and with his true intentions made clear, your annoyance quickly disappeared. You playfully punched Jumin in the shoulder.

“You should’ve just said that from the beginning.”

He looked away and blushed, folding his arms and pouting.

“Are you blushing?!?!?”

“Hmph.”

“Stop being so pouty and cute. I’ll stay, I’ll stay!” you poked and pestered him until he turned to face you again, “I’ll stay okay?” His face broke into a grin and he pulled you into a warm embrace.

Bringing his face close to your ear, he whispered, “You should shower. Your clothes are soaking wet from all the snow.”

You felt butterflies in your stomach and stood abruptly. “Hey,” you pointed in his face, “calm down before I change my mind and call a taxi.”

“Alright, Alright,” Jumin put his hands in the air to show his innocence but the mischievous look in his eyes said otherwise. “There are some clean towels and a robe in the bathroom. You can leave your wet clothes in the hamper.”
