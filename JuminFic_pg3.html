“I’ll have my chefs do that for you, I know they won’t mind.”

“But they don’t have my mom’s recipes.”

“Then write them down,” he retorted, leaning into you even more.

You glared at him and tried to push him off. Did this man have a comeback for everything?

“Actually, I didn’t even finish decorating my house and I planned to do that when I got back home tonight, so I should get going.”

“Y/N you live alone, that’s so unnecessary. If you want a decorated house, just stay here and look at mine.”

You only just noticed the massive, brightly lit Christmas tree sitting in the corner of the living room and white fairy lights strung across the walls and bordering the doorframes.

“Ohh they’re nice,” you were momentarily mesmerized until, “...Wait! You live alone too so why are you allowed to decorate but I’m not?!”

“Y/N first of all, I don’t live alone, I live with Elizabeth III and--” as if on cue, the elegant white cat slinked into the room and curled up at Jumin’s socked feet, “--second of all, I didn’t put these up, my housekeepers did.”

You huffed, irritated at the fact that your boyfriend’s quick wit and smart mouth shutdown everything you said, but you weren’t about to let him win.

“My mom will get worried.”

“Tell her you’re with me. She loves me.”

“I left my stove on.”

“Liar,” he wrapped his arms around you and wouldn’t budge. “Accept defeat.”

“Babe! Why do you want me to sleep over tonight? You’re usually so strict about this kind of thing...”
